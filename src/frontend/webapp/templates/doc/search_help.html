You can filter the table of variants by:
  • A gene name (or unique alias) or HGVS-ID. You may preprend HGNC (e. g. BARD1)
  • A HGVS c. string. Beware that you must specify a transcript for this to work (e. g. ENST00000260947:c.1972C>T)
  • A chromosomal range of the form: chr:start-end (e. g. chr2:214767531-214780740)
  • A consensus classification
If multiple constraints are selected they are joint with 'AND'. This means that if you choose to search for BARD1 in the genes and select consensus classification 1 you will get all variants from BARD1 which are classified with 1.
You can separate multiple genes, HGVS or chromosomal ranges in their respective fields either with semicolons (';'), commas (','), tabulators or newlines. It is also possible to have a combination of them.
Meaning of the classification:
  • 1: Benign
  • 2: Likely benign
  • 3: Uncertain significance
  • 4: Likely pathogenic
  • 5: Pathogenic
